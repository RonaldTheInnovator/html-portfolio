Best Movies According to Ronald Netya

Top 3 Movies of all-time

Mission Impossible 3

In this high-octane third installment of the Mission: Impossible series, IMF agent Ethan Hunt (Tom Cruise) comes out of semi-retirement to rescue a captured protégé and face off against a ruthless arms dealer, Owen Davian (Philip Seymour Hoffman). As the stakes escalate, Hunt must navigate betrayal, intense action sequences, and global espionage while trying to protect his fiancée, Julia (Michelle Monaghan), from being caught in the crossfire. With pulse-pounding thrills, emotional stakes, and standout performances, Mission: Impossible III blends personal drama with explosive action in a race against time to stop a devastating weapon from falling into the wrong hands.

Casino

Casino is a gripping crime drama based on real events, following the rise and fall of Sam "Ace" Rothstein (Robert De Niro), a gambling expert handpicked by the mob to run a glitzy Las Vegas casino during the 1970s. As he builds a successful empire, his world begins to unravel due to his volatile friend and enforcer Nicky Santoro (Joe Pesci), and his turbulent relationship with hustler-turned-socialite Ginger McKenna (Sharon Stone). A story of greed, power, betrayal, and corruption, Casino dives deep into the dark underbelly of organized crime and the high-stakes world of Las Vegas. With Scorsese’s signature style, intense performances, and a powerful soundtrack, the film is a cinematic classic.

21

Inspired by a true story, 21 follows Ben Campbell (Jim Sturgess), a brilliant MIT student who joins a secret group of students trained by their professor (Kevin Spacey) to count cards and win big in Las Vegas. Using fake identities and elaborate strategies, the team turns blackjack into a high-stakes operation, making millions in winnings. As Ben becomes more drawn to the money, the lifestyle, and his teammate Jill (Kate Bosworth), the risks escalate. With casino security closing in and loyalties being tested, he soon finds himself in a dangerous game that's much more than just cards. 21 is a slick, fast-paced thriller about brains, ambition, and the price of getting in too deep.
